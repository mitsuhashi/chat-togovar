### English:
The single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) **rs796052984** is a variant located in the **TYR gene**, which encodes tyrosinase, an enzyme involved in melanin biosynthesis. Below is the information related to its allele frequency in European populations:

1. **Variant Identification**:
   - **rs number**: rs796052984
   - **HGVS notation**: NM_000372.5:c.1205G>A (p.Arg402Gln)
   - **Gene**: TYR (Tyrosinase)
   - **Transcript**: ENST00000263377.9
   - [dbSNP link](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/snp/rs796052984)

2. **Allele Frequency in European Populations**:
   - According to the **gnomAD v3.1.2 database**, the allele frequency of rs796052984 in **European (non-Finnish)** populations is approximately **0.0001 (0.01%)**. In **Finnish Europeans**, the frequency is slightly higher, around **0.0003 (0.03%)**.
   - This variant is rare in European populations and is classified as a low-frequency variant.

3. **Disease Associations**:
   - This variant has been associated with **oculocutaneous albinism type 1 (OCA1)** in a recessive inheritance pattern. It is considered a hypomorphic allele, meaning it reduces but does not completely eliminate tyrosinase activity.

4. **Literature Evidence**:
   - Studies have reported that rs796052984 (p.Arg402Gln) is a temperature-sensitive variant, leading to reduced tyrosinase activity at lower temperatures. This has been linked to mild pigmentation phenotypes and albinism in compound heterozygous individuals. Relevant publications include:
     - Tripathi et al., 1992, *American Journal of Human Genetics* (PMID: 1352887)
     - Simeonov et al., 2013, *Human Mutation* (PMID: 23364476)

5. **GWAS Findings**:
   - No specific GWAS studies have directly linked rs796052984 to phenotypes beyond its known role in pigmentation and albinism.

6. **Allele Frequency Comparison**:
   - In comparison, the allele frequency in **East Asian populations** (including Japanese) is even lower, typically below 0.00005. This suggests that rs796052984 is more prevalent in European populations than in East Asian populations.
