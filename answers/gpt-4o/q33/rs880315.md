#### 2. Disease Associations
The rs880315 variant has been studied in the context of autoimmune diseases, particularly **type 1 diabetes** and **celiac disease**, due to its location in the HLA-DQB1 gene. Variants in this region are known to influence splicing regulation, which can alter the expression of HLA-DQB1 isoforms. Aberrant splicing may impact antigen presentation and immune system function, contributing to disease susceptibility.

Predictive tools such as **SpliceAI** suggest that rs880315 may have a moderate impact on splicing regulation, but experimental validation is required to confirm this.

- No direct experimental evidence for rs880315's effect on splicing regulation is currently available.

#### 5. GWAS Findings
