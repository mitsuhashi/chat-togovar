### English:
The single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) **rs796052984** is located in the **ALDH2** gene, which encodes the enzyme aldehyde dehydrogenase 2. This enzyme plays a critical role in the metabolism of alcohol by converting acetaldehyde, a toxic intermediate produced during alcohol metabolism, into acetate, a less harmful substance. The rs796052984 variant is associated with the **ALDH2*2** allele, which results in a missense mutation (Glu504Lys or E504K). This mutation significantly reduces the enzymatic activity of ALDH2.

Individuals carrying the rs796052984 variant (especially homozygous carriers) have impaired acetaldehyde metabolism, leading to the accumulation of acetaldehyde after alcohol consumption. This can cause symptoms such as facial flushing, nausea, and an increased heart rate, commonly referred to as "alcohol flush reaction." Additionally, the accumulation of acetaldehyde is associated with an increased risk of alcohol-related cancers, such as esophageal cancer.

Regarding drug metabolism, the reduced activity of ALDH2 can also affect the metabolism of certain drugs and chemicals that are detoxified by ALDH2. For example:
- **Nitroglycerin**: ALDH2 is involved in the bioactivation of nitroglycerin, a drug used to treat angina. Individuals with the rs796052984 variant may have reduced therapeutic efficacy of nitroglycerin.
- **Cyclophosphamide**: ALDH2 also plays a role in detoxifying aldehyde byproducts of drugs like cyclophosphamide, a chemotherapy agent. Impaired ALDH2 activity may increase the risk of side effects.

In summary, rs796052984 affects both alcohol metabolism and the metabolism of certain drugs, leading to clinical implications that vary depending on the individual's genotype.
