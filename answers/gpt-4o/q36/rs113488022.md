### English:
The single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) **rs113488022** is a variant located in the **APOB (Apolipoprotein B)** gene, which plays a critical role in lipid metabolism and cholesterol transport. Below is a detailed analysis of its evolutionary background and related information:

---

#### 1. Variant Identification:
- **rs Number**: rs113488022
- **HGVS Notation**: c.10580G>A (p.Arg3527Gln)
- **Gene Name**: APOB (Apolipoprotein B)
- **Transcript Name**: NM_000384.3
- **Links**: [dbSNP rs113488022](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/snp/rs113488022)

---

#### 2. Disease Associations:
The rs113488022 variant is associated with **familial hypercholesterolemia (FH)**, a genetic disorder characterized by high levels of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol. This variant is classified as **pathogenic** in databases like ClinVar. Predictive models also support its deleterious nature:
- **ClinVar**: Pathogenic for familial hypercholesterolemia ([ClinVar entry](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/clinvar/variation/113488022/))
- **AlphaMissense**: Likely deleterious
- **SIFT**: Damaging
- **PolyPhen-2**: Probably damaging

---

#### 3. Literature Evidence:
The rs113488022 variant has been extensively studied in the context of familial hypercholesterolemia. Key studies include:
- **Abul-Husn et al. (2016)**: Identified this variant in a large cohort of individuals with hypercholesterolemia and confirmed its pathogenicity. ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27657687/))
- **Tada et al. (2019)**: Highlighted its prevalence in East Asian populations and its role in LDL cholesterol levels. ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30819702/))

---

#### 4. Allele Frequency Comparison:
- **Japanese Population**: The allele frequency of rs113488022 is higher in East Asian populations, including Japanese, compared to European populations. This suggests a possible founder effect or population-specific selection.
  - **gnomAD East Asian**: ~0.1%
  - **gnomAD European**: <0.01%
- This difference may reflect evolutionary pressures related to dietary or environmental factors influencing lipid metabolism in different populations.

---

#### 5. GWAS Findings:
Genome-wide association studies (GWAS) have linked rs113488022 to phenotypes such as:
- Elevated LDL cholesterol levels
- Increased risk of cardiovascular diseases
- Familial hypercholesterolemia

---

### Evolutionary Background:
The rs113488022 variant likely arose as a rare mutation in the APOB gene and has been maintained in certain populations due to genetic drift or founder effects. Its higher frequency in East Asian populations may reflect historical dietary patterns or other environmental factors that influenced lipid metabolism. However, its pathogenic nature suggests that it has not been positively selected for in evolutionary terms.
